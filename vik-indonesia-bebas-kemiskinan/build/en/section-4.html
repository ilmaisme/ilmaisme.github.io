Lessons for All

Back
WVI’s experience in 6 districts in East Nusa Tenggara and West Kalimantan offers valuable lessons on how to provide water and sanitation for all.
First, the role of the community is essential.
To make this work, Village-Owned Enterprises are needed. They can expand access to funding, allow for legal income, and build more sustainable financial systems. Communities must also be empowered to manage these enterprises effectively.
Everyone needs to collaborate and strengthen each other—from village communities to district, provincial, and national governments.
With toolkits, roadmaps, and financial reporting platforms, sustainable water and sanitation management can be achieved.
Interested in making this happen in your area? WVI’s report and policy brief can serve as your guide.
