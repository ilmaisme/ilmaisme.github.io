Since 2022, Wahana Visi Indonesia (WVI) has been running FinWASH4UC (Financing Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene for Universal Coverage) project.

But financing here isn’t just about building pipes. It’s about something more fundamental: governance.

The project is a co-creation in spirit, meaning it is designed together. First, stakeholders were asked to evaluate the problems themselves. It became clear: water scarcity is about weak commitments, limited funds, lack of understanding, inactive working groups, poor planning platforms, and non-inclusive infrastructure.

Toolkit

Toolkits were introduced. Local governments and working groups co-designed 5 practical toolkits to identify water and sanitation budgets. Those five are rainwater harvesting tanks, gravity-fed systems, toilets & septic tanks, borehole systems, handwashing facilities

“This toolkit makes quick calculations easier for villages. Before, we had to pay consultants. Now we can estimate costs ourselves, though some tools still need fine-tuning.” - Vicky Nggae, Chair of KPS-PAM Ngada
“We thought our budgeting tools were advanced. But this toolkit is smarter. It allows us to predict to predict population growth and water needs, beyond infrastructure.” - Hermanus Batu, Head of Water Division, PUPR En
“With this toolkit, it is expected that village governments will find it easier to calculate the costs required to build water and sanitation infrastructure that supports SDG 6 in Melawi.” — Paulus, Secretary of Melawi Regency
Roadmap
Villages and districts co-created roadmaps, which include current conditions of water and sanitation, targets, financing needs, available funds, and gaps to be filled locally. By 2023, six districts had successfully designed their own roadmap.
“These roadmaps help BAPPEDA and the Pokja PKP align their plans for drinking water and sanitation programs across local government agencies (OPDs), and also serve as a guide for village governments in preparing WASH budgets in the Village Budget.”- Andreas Worho, Head of Bappeda Ende

Citizen Journalism and Trainings
Around 123 adults and 107 children were trained to tell water stories, raising awareness. It is hoped that the stories they produce will build awareness of water and sanitation issues.

Patnership
Knowing that village funds and government budgets are limited, WVI trained 578 stakeholders on investment and partnerships in water and sanitation governance. WVI also encouraged stakeholders to develop networks with financial institutions, from banks to cooperatives, so that stakeholders could better understand funding opportunities as well as the terms and conditions involved.

These efforts paid off. Bank NTT eventually launched a new product called the Water and Sanitation Supply System Credit (KSPAMS). Bank Kalimantan Barat also introduced the Drinking Water and Sanitation Credit (KREASI). Beyond banks, cooperatives such as Sangosay in NTT, along with Credit Union Keling Kumang and Lantang Tipo in West Kalimantan, also joined the network.

Efficiency and Transparency
Only with efficient and transparent financial processes can water flow to everyone. That’s why village governments were also encouraged to understand and further develop the Village Financial System (Siskeudes) reporting platform, transitioning it into digital form.

WVI trained 578 government staff, community members, and partners in using Siskeudes. With a digitized reporting system, village governments can input data without needing to go to the district office and can detect problems in planning at an earlier stage. Villages also become more orderly in their administration.

“While at Bappeda, I was involved in discussions about the server. After moving to Komdigi, I immediately coordinated with DPMD to move the server to the Komdigi office, in line with our mandate to manage IT. If there are internet network issues, we will look for solutions at the sub-district office so that data input can still be done online. This support is very important to accelerate digitalization and public services in Ende District.”— Herman Sigasare, Secretary of the Communication and Digital Affairs Department (Komdigi), Ende District.
“For planning, budgeting, and reporting, data can be entered directly through the online Siskeudes system. This process can be done directly from the village, and if there are any issues, colleagues from the sub-district office who have also been trained by WVI—will provide assistance. This way, there is no need to waste time and money traveling to the district office.” — Felicytas Don Bosco, Super Admin of DPMD, Ende Distric.
Policy
Finally, communities were empowered not just to manage water — but to shape policy. They voiced their concerns directly to policymakers.
